Mandela's Life

  • 1918: Nelson Mandela is born in Mvezo, South Africa.
  • 1944: Mandela joins the African National Congress (ANC).
  • 1952: Mandela becomes the ANC's deputy national president.
  • 1962: Mandela is arrested and sentenced to five years in prison for inciting a strike and leaving the country without permission.
  • 1964: Mandela is sentenced to life imprisonment for sabotage and conspiracy against the South African government.
  • 1990: After 27 years of imprisonment, Mandela is released from Victor Verster Prison.
  • 1993: Mandela is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize, along with then-South African President F.W. de Klerk, for their efforts to dismantle apartheid.
  • 1994: Mandela becomes the first black president of South Africa in the country's first multiracial elections.
  • 1995: Mandela establishes the Truth and Reconciliation Commission to address the crimes committed during apartheid.
  • 1999: Mandela steps down as president of South Africa, allowing for a peaceful transition of power.
  • 2009: Mandela celebrates his 91st birthday and his legacy is commemorated as Nelson Mandela International Day, recognized by the United Nations.
